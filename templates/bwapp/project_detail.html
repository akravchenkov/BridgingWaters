Post-Project Maintenance

The women's committee manages and maintains the pool. They insure the cleanliness. If construction assistance is needed for the maintenance the men of the village provide this.

Responsibilities: The Women's Committee
Funding: None was needed.
When did the community take ownership: The village felt ownership through constructing the facility.

Lessons Learned

Social centers and water conservation is a positive contribution to the village.

Successes: A pool was created where laundry could be done. For an area that depends on rainwater collection, this helps to conserve water. Also, it provided a central location for children to play during the heat of the day.
Obstacles: The most difficult part was getting the project going. It was also difficult to express the need for this pool to outside groups.
Community Impact: The women of the village spend time socially doing laundry and bathing. The children enjoy this pool and can be supervised by adults.
Pre-Construction Actions Required by the Community: The need from the program was discovered by interviewing citizens of the village. The results of this were then submitted to the village counsel.
